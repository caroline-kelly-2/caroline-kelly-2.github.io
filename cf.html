changing futures
Tusla, the child welfare agency, asked us to create a website that informs young people about what Tusla does.


They found that children who regularly used TUSLA’s various services often couldn’t understand the language used on their main website or found it hard to find and access the information they needed.

We were introduced to a number of children from 10-17 which we met up and talked with over the course of this project. By talking with them we discovered the key pain points, and their main goals when they’re looking for information (whether is be on what a social worker is, on what going to court is like etc). Tusla wanted a website which would be made with these users in mind. They wanted them to understand more about what was going on around them and empower them.
